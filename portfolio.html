Key Projects

Face Liveness Authentication

Objective:

Develop a secure, real-time face liveness detection system to prevent spoofing attacks (e.g., photos, videos, masks) during biometric authentication.

Approach:

  • Implemented deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) trained on large proprietary datasets to distinguish live faces from presentation attacks.
  • Integrated optical flow analysis and 3D geometry estimation to detect subtle differences between live faces and static images or masks.
  • Combined texture analysis and motion cues for enhanced anti-spoofing accuracy.

Results:

  • Achieved high accuracy in detecting spoofing attempts under varied lighting and device conditions.
  • System integrated with mobile onboarding and authentication workflows, significantly reducing fraud risk.
Voice Liveness Authentication

Objective:

Add voice-based liveness detection to strengthen multi-factor biometric authentication.

Approach:

  • Developed machine learning models to analyze speech patterns and detect replay attacks or synthetic audio.
  • Fused face and voice liveness checks for exponential improvement in spoof prevention.

Results:

  • Enabled seamless, passive user experience with high resistance to impersonation and identity concealment attempts.
  • Supported onboarding and secure access in regulated environments.
